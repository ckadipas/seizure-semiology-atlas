# CORPUS EXTRACTION SUMMARY (source-grounded figures)

## Loddenkemper 2005 (Epilepsy&Behav) — lateralizing-signs review, Table 1
- Unilateral sensory aura: 6.1% pts, 89% contra, BA1-3
- Hemifield visual aura: 28.6% OLE, 100% contra, BA17-19
- Version: 22.2% FLE, 100% contra, BA6/8
- Clonic: 44.4% FLE, 83% contra, BA4/6
- Tonic: 48.1% FLE, 89% contra, SMA/BA6
- Figure-of-4: 17.7% TLE/15% ETLE, 89% contra, SMA/prefrontal
- Unilateral dystonic posturing: 43.9% TLE, ~100% contra (1 exception), basal ganglia
- Automatisms + preserved responsiveness: 5.7% TLE, ~100% non-dominant
- Ictal spitting: 0.3% EMU, 75% non-dominant
- Ictal vomiting: 2% EMU, 81% non-dominant (right)
- Unilateral eye-blinking: 1.5% EMU, 83% ipsilateral
- Ictal speech (preserved): 34.2% EMU, 83% non-dominant
- Ictal dysphasia/aphasia: 34.2% EMU, 100% dominant
- Postictal (Todd) palsy: 0.6% EMU, 93% contra
- Postictal nose-wiping: 53.2% TLE, 92% ipsilateral

## Foldvary-Schaefer 2009/2011 (Epilepsy&Behav)
- Abdominal aura -> TLE 74%; +automotor -> 98%
- Limb dystonia contralateral >90%; version contralateral; rotation w/ version=contra, w/o version=ipsi

## Marashly 2015 — ictal motor SEQUENCES, PPV analysis
- Reliable (PPV>80%): version, unilateral tonic posturing, M2e, unilateral clonic
- Figure-of-4 & hand dystonia: PPV<80% in this cohort (CAUTION/discordance)
- >=2 reliable signs same side -> lateralization accuracy 100%

## Kotagal 2000 — asymmetric tonic limb posturing (figure-of-4) in 2-deg GTC
- Extended elbow contra: 94.4% (group II), 83-88% (group I); version contra 100%
- ATLP present: 75% TLE, 53% XTLE of 2-deg GTC

## Gabr 1989 (Ann Neurol) — speech
- Postictal dysphasia -> dominant 92% (p<0.001); ictal preserved speech -> non-dominant 83%

## Roh 1996 (OCR) — ictal behaviors TLE
- Forced version contra 89%; unilateral eye-blink & automatism ipsilateral ~85%
